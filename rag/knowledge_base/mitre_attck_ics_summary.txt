MITRE ATT&CK for ICS - technique summary notes

Source: MITRE ATT&CK for ICS matrix, publicly available at attack.mitre.org. This file
contains original summary notes for RAG indexing, not a reproduction of MITRE's text.

Techniques relevant to the attack classes GridGuard AI detects:

T0814 - Denial of Service: Adversaries may flood ICS network communications (e.g. MMS
requests to an RTU or gateway) to degrade or block availability of control/monitoring
functions. Typical indicators: sustained spike in request rate, reduced packet size,
increased latency on the targeted asset.

T0830 - Man in the Middle: Adversaries position themselves between two ICS components to
intercept or alter traffic in transit. Typical indicators: elevated and inconsistent
latency/jitter, traffic appearing to originate from or route through an unexpected asset.

T0856 - Spoof Reporting Message / False Data Injection: Adversaries manipulate reported
process values to mislead operators or downstream automated control logic. Typical
indicators: reported values statistically inconsistent with sensor physics or historical
baselines, often concentrated in Write/Report/SampledValues service calls.

T0851 - Rogue Master / T0857 - System Firmware (context) and Replay-style techniques:
Adversaries capture and resend legitimate protocol messages to trigger unintended actions
or maintain a false appearance of normal operation. Typical indicators: near-duplicate
payloads, unnaturally regular inter-arrival timing, high duplicate ratio.

General ICS defense notes: asset criticality should factor into response prioritization -
protection relays and breakers (e.g. IED-Breaker-*) generally carry higher operational
impact than monitoring-only assets (e.g. Substation-HMI) if compromised.
